    structural glazing

    Structural glazing is a modern facade system where glass is attached directly to the building's structure using specialized silicone sealants or adhesives, creating a sleek, frameless look.

    curtain walls

    Curtain walls are non-load-bearing exterior facades made of lightweight materials like glass and aluminum, designed to enhance a building's aesthetics while providing insulation and protection from weather.

    semi-structural glazing

    façade system that combines sleek aesthetics with strong structural performance by using partially hidden frames to support glass panels. This design offers a modern, elegant look while maintaining durability and weather resistance

    skylight

    Skylights are roof-mounted windows designed to bring natural light into buildings, enhancing brightness and energy efficiency. They improve indoor ambiance, reduce artificial lighting needs, and can provide ventilation in some designs.

    Aluminum Cladding

    Aluminum Cladding (A.C.P) involves using composite aluminum panels to enhance building exteriors with a modern, durable, and weather-resistant finish. It offers thermal and acoustic insulation, lightweight properties, and flexible design options for various architectural styles.

    Aluminum Louvres

    Aluminum Louvres are architectural systems designed to enhance ventilation, control natural light, and reduce direct sunlight exposure. They combine functionality with modern aesthetics, offering durability and energy efficiency for various applications.

    UPVC

    UPVC (Unplasticized Polyvinyl Chloride) is a durable, low-maintenance material widely used for doors and windows, offering excellent thermal and acoustic insulation. It is weather-resistant, eco-friendly, and available in various designs to suit modern architectural needs.

    Spider Glazing

    Spider glazing is a frameless glass facade system that uses point-fixed fittings for support, creating a sleek and modern architectural appearance. It allows maximum natural light while ensuring durability and resistance to environmental pressures.

    Tempered Glass-Securit

    Tempered glass, also known as securit glass, is a safety glass that undergoes heat treatment to increase its strength. It is designed to shatter into small, harmless pieces upon impact, making it ideal for applications requiring durability and safety, such as windows, facades, and doors.

    Aluminum Doors and Windows

    Aluminum doors and windows are durable, lightweight, and corrosion-resistant, making them ideal for modern architectural designs. They offer excellent thermal and sound insulation, customizable styles, and low maintenance, ensuring long-lasting performance.
